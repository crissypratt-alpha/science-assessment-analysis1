Key Findings

What the analysis revealed about Alpha's MAP readiness
  • Alpha tests only 54% of what MAP expects. 104 of 228 NGSS standards have zero questions on our mastery tests.
  • The gap is concentrated in middle school. Grades 3–5 are in relatively good shape (71–100% coverage). Grades 6–8 cover only 34–53%.
  • Engineering/Technology is a blind spot. ETS standards are completely untested and mostly untaught in Grades 4–8.
  • Curriculum gaps mirror the testing gaps. 38 standards have no curriculum at all; 112 have only weak/tangential coverage.
  • 37 tested standards show below-75% accuracy — students are being taught and tested but not mastering the material.

The Mismatch

The majority of our test questions are at the Scripted or Low Guided complexity levels (basic recall and simple application). MAP items operate at High Guided or Doing Science levels requiring multi-step reasoning, evidence-based argumentation, and sense-making. Even when we test the right standard, students who score well on our low-complexity questions may still struggle with MAP.

See the Test Quality Dashboard for question-by-question complexity scores across all 83 tests.

Bottom Line

  • Grades 3–5 are in solid shape with only minor gaps to fill.
  • The critical problem is Grades 6–8 where two-thirds of MAP standards go untested.
  • Engineering/Technology is completely absent from testing and mostly absent from curriculum in all middle school grades.
  • Physical Science has the widest gap — forces, energy, waves, and matter conservation standards are largely untested and weakly taught.
  • The highest-impact actions are: (1) addressing the ETS blind spot, (2) adding Physical Science content in G6–8, and (3) upgrading test question complexity.
