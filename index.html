Key Findings

What the analysis revealed about Alpha's MAP readiness
  • Our questions are far too simple. Average complexity is 1.96 out of 5.0 — MAP expects 3.0+. 42% of our questions are basic recall ("Scripted"), while MAP has virtually none.
  • Only 1.2% of questions reach "Doing Science" level. Just 36 of 3,093 questions ask students to design investigations, build models, or argue from evidence — exactly what MAP tests.
  • CCC and SEP scores are critically low. Crosscutting Concepts average 1.76/5.0 and Science & Engineering Practices average 1.81/5.0 — the two dimensions MAP weighs most heavily.
  • Alpha tests only 54% of what MAP expects. 104 of 228 NGSS standards have zero questions. The gap is concentrated in Grades 6–8 (34–53% coverage).
  • Engineering/Technology is a blind spot. ETS standards are completely untested and mostly untaught in Grades 4–8.
  • Curriculum gaps mirror the testing gaps. 38 standards have no curriculum at all; 112 have only weak/tangential coverage.
  • 37 tested standards show below-75% accuracy — students are being taught and tested but not mastering the material.

Part 1: Question Complexity — The Biggest Gap

3,093 questions evaluated across 100 tests using the Achieve Assessment Framework — our tests are dramatically less complex than what MAP requires

🚨 The Core Problem: Our Questions Are Too Easy

Across all 3,093 questions, the average cognitive complexity is 1.96 out of 5.0. MAP Science items typically require 3.0+ complexity. This means even when we test the right standard, our questions don't prepare students for the rigor MAP demands — multi-step reasoning, evidence-based argumentation, and real-world sense-making.

What Do These Levels Mean?

Scripted — "What is photosynthesis?" Basic recall, one-step answer, no context needed. MAP has almost none of these.
Low Guided — "Which plant grew tallest?" Simple data reading from a provided table. MAP has some, but paired with deeper follow-ups.
High Guided — "Use the data to explain why Plant B grew faster." Multi-step reasoning with evidence. This is MAP's sweet spot.
Doing Science — "Design an experiment to test whether light color affects growth." Open-ended investigation, models, argumentation.

Prioritized Action Plan

What to do first, second, and ongoing
Immediate — Before MAP Testing
1. Reteach the 10 urgent standards where students score below 50%
2. Begin upgrading Scripted questions — prioritize the 1,296 recall-only questions, starting with G4 and G5 where 50% and 45% are Scripted. Add scenarios, data tables, and multi-step reasoning.
3. Write Engineering/Technology test questions for G4–G8 — ETS1-1 through ETS1-4 are completely missing
This Semester
4. Systematically raise CCC and SEP scores — these two indicators (1.76 and 1.81) are the biggest complexity gaps. Every new or revised question should include crosscutting concepts and science practices.
5. Write the 104 missing standard test questions at High Guided complexity from the start (don't create more Scripted questions)
6. Add the 38 missing curriculum lessons and strengthen the 112 weakly-covered standards
Ongoing
7. Target 3.0+ average complexity per grade — currently every grade is below 2.2. Convert remaining Low Guided questions to High Guided by adding data interpretation and evidence-based reasoning.
8. Re-evaluate quarterly — re-run this analysis as new tests and lessons are added to track progress toward MAP-level complexity

Bottom Line

  • Alpha has two problems, not one. It's not just that we test only 54% of standards — the questions we do have are at barely 40% of the complexity MAP requires (1.96 vs. 3.0+).
  • 42% of all questions are basic recall (Scripted level). MAP has virtually zero questions at this level. Students practicing on our tests aren't building the reasoning skills MAP demands.
  • CCC and SEP are the biggest weaknesses — averaging 1.76 and 1.81 out of 5.0. Questions almost never ask students to identify patterns, think about systems, or use science practices.
  • Grades 6–8 have a double problem: missing standards (34–53% coverage) AND low complexity. Engineering/Technology is completely absent.
  • The highest-impact actions are: (1) rewrite existing Scripted questions to add scenarios, data, and reasoning, (2) add Engineering/Technology standards across G4–8, and (3) build out missing middle school standards with High Guided complexity from the start.
