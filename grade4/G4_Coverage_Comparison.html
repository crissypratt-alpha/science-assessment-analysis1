Key Takeaways

MAP Science covers everything NGSS requires
MAP Science 3-5 is aligned to ALL 16 Grade 4 NGSS performance expectations (plus Grades 3 and 5). It is adaptive and draws from the full standard. Students will encounter questions on every domain and standard.
Skill plan is strong with 128 lessons across all domains
11 of 16 PEs are fully taught with 128 lessons spanning Life Science (42), Earth Science (33), Physical Science (42), and bonus Space content (7). The plan goes beyond NGSS with extra space science content not required at Grade 4.
G4.1 test has significant gaps: 6 of 16 standards untested
Six standards get zero questions: collisions/energy change (4-PS3-3), energy conversion devices (4-PS3-4), Earth features from maps (4-ESS2-2), natural hazard solutions (4-ESS3-2), comparing solutions (3-5-ETS1-2), and fair testing (3-5-ETS1-3). Students could pass this test and still bomb MAP on these topics.
G4.1 test is badly imbalanced: 45% on just 2 standards
14 of 31 questions (45%) cover just 2 standards: rock layers/fossils (4-ESS1-1, 7 Qs) and energy transfer (4-PS3-2, 7 Qs). Meanwhile 6 entire standards get zero questions. This provides almost no diagnostic value for the majority of the curriculum.
Engineering design nearly absent from the test
Only 1 question (Q26) addresses engineering design. Two of three ETS standards are completely untested (comparing solutions, fair testing). The skill plan also only partially addresses engineering. MAP will test all three ETS standards — students need more explicit practice.
Physical science undertested despite strong skill plan
The skill plan has 42 Physical Science lessons, yet 4-PS3-3 (collisions) and 4-PS3-4 (energy conversion devices) are both completely untested on G4.1 and only partially covered in lessons. Students could miss these entirely on MAP with no warning from the Alpha test.
