Key Takeaways

MAP Science 6-8 covers all MS PEs
MAP Science 6-8 is aligned to ALL 18 middle school NGSS performance expectations. Note: MS standards are banded across grades 6-8, not grade-specific. The adaptive assessment draws from the full standard across the band.
G8.1 is the BEST test of all grades tested
42 questions covering 16 of 18 PEs makes G8.1 the most comprehensive test across all grades analyzed. Only 2 standards are missing (Earth surface processes and engineering design), compared to 6+ missing in lower grades.
Strong genetics/heredity coverage aligns with G8 curriculum
11+ questions on genetics, Punnett squares, mutations, and heredity (MS-LS3-1 and MS-LS3-2) align well with the Grade 8 curriculum focus. This is the deepest coverage of any single domain area across all grade tests.
Earth science well-represented across multiple topics
Groundwater resources (3 Qs), natural hazards (3 Qs), climate change/human impact (3 Qs), geologic time (4 Qs), and plate tectonics (1 Q) give broad Earth science coverage. 5 of 7 Earth PEs are tested.
Engineering design STILL absent — pattern across ALL grades
0 of 2 ETS standards are tested on G8.1. This is consistent with the same gap in Grades 3, 4, 6, and 7. Engineering design is systematically missing from every Alpha test, yet MAP will assess it. This is the single most persistent gap across the test suite.
Genetics-heavy but less imbalanced than lower grades
11 of 42 questions (26%) cover heredity/Punnett squares, and 5 questions (12%) cover kinetic energy. While there is some clustering, the overall distribution is more balanced than lower-grade tests, which concentrated 50%+ of questions on 2-3 standards.
