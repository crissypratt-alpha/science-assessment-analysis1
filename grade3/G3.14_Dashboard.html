What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK Level 1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Student applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Student applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. No single right path. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

Issues with construct redundancy and topic balance
  • Life Cycle Recall — 3 questions (Q1, Q2, Q3)
    Three life cycle questions at varying complexity. Q1 is pure recall (identical to G3.13 Q1). Keep Q2, redesign Q3, remove Q1.
  • Plant Anatomy Recall — 2 questions (Q21, Q22)
    Both test plant structure/function at pure recall level. Combine into one data-driven question.
  • ETS Under-represented
    Only 2 ETS questions (9%) vs. 17% NGSS target. Use freed slots to add engineering design items.
  • G3.14 is a Parallel Version of G3.13
    Same topic structure, same question types, same strong items (climate data graphs, engineering design). Consider differentiating these tests or combining their best elements.

Notable Improvement: Q16 and Q17 Are Stronger Than G3.13 Equivalents

The toy car experiment (Q16) provides actual observation data, and the charged ball question (Q17) requires applying charge rules to explain observations — both are upgrades over G3.13's recall versions of the same concepts.
