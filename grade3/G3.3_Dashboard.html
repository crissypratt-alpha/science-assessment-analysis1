Achieve Standard: 0% DOK1 Items

This assessment has 66% DOK1 items — the highest Scripted rate of any G3 test evaluated. Two-thirds of the questions are pure recall. With only 2 High Guided questions (both involving experimental magnet data) and no Doing Science items, this test fails to measure scientific reasoning for most of its length. The avg complexity of 1.63 is the lowest of any Grade 3 test. However, Q2 and Q30 demonstrate that even this test can produce strong data-driven items when experimental scenarios are used.

What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK Level 1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Student applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Student applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. No single right path. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

Issues with construct redundancy and topic balance
  • Extreme Scripted Overrepresentation (66% — more than double the 20-30% target)
    Two-thirds of the assessment is pure recall. This is the highest Scripted rate of any G3 test evaluated.
  • No ETS/Engineering Questions (0% vs. 17% target)
    Zero engineering design items. An entire NGSS domain is completely absent from this assessment.
  • ESS Severely Underrepresented (9% vs. 17% target)
    Only 3 ESS questions, all at Scripted level. Need at least 2-3 more with higher complexity.
  • Multiple Redundant Recall Items
    Q7/Q8 (True/False and list selection about inheritance/life cycles), Q10/Q11 (both test weather/climate definitions), Q13/Q14 (both about trait variation at recall level), Q18/Q26 (both gravity recall), Q20/Q24 (both magnet force recall). These redundancies waste assessment slots.
  • Technical Issue: Non-Standard Answer Choices
    Q7 and Q13 have only 2-3 answer choices instead of the standard 4, reducing psychometric reliability.

MAP Alignment: Before & After Redesigns
1
Q7 — True/False About Inheritance
Replace True/False format with evidence-based analysis.
Before: “True or False: Children always show all of their parents traits.”
After: “A student collected data on eye color from 10 families [data table showing parent and child eye colors]. Based on this data, do children always have the same eye color as their parents? Use evidence from the table to support your answer.”
Scripted → High Guided
2
Q10 — Weather Forecast Recall
Replace definition recall with data application.
Before: “Scientists record patterns of weather across different times and areas so that they can make...”
After: “A meteorologist collected 5 days of temperature, wind, and pressure data [data table]. Based on the pattern in the data, predict tomorrow’s most likely weather conditions. Explain how you used the data to make your prediction.”
Scripted → High Guided
3
Q18 — Gravity Recall
Replace recall with data-driven investigation.
Before: “If you drop an object off a roof and it falls downward, this is because of...”
After: “Students dropped balls of different masses from the same height and recorded the time each took to reach the ground [data table]. Based on the data, what pattern do you notice? What does this tell you about how gravity affects objects of different masses?”
Scripted → High Guided
4
Q8 — Life Cycle Recall
Replace with comparative analysis.
Before: “Which statement is true about life cycles?”
After: “Here are life cycle diagrams for three different organisms [images]. Compare the life cycles and identify: (1) Which stages are common to all three? (2) Which organism has the most different life cycle? Use evidence from the diagrams.”
Scripted → High Guided
5
Q26 — Gravity Facts
Replace with experimental analysis.
Before: “Which of these statements are true about the force of gravity?”
After: “Students rolled a ball up a ramp and recorded its speed at different heights [data table]. Based on the data, describe what happens to the ball’s speed as it goes up. What force is causing this change? Use the data to support your explanation.”
Scripted → High Guided
6
Q24 — Magnet Distance
Replace with data interpretation.
Before: “Which is true about magnet distance and force?”
After: “Students measured how many paper clips a magnet could pick up at different distances [data: 1cm=15, 3cm=8, 5cm=3, 10cm=0]. Graph the data and describe the pattern. Predict how many clips could be picked up at 7cm.”
Scripted → Doing Science
7
Q11 — Climate Definition
Replace with data analysis.
Before: “An area’s typical weather conditions over time is known as...”
After: “Here are two data sets: Set A shows temperature readings for one week. Set B shows average monthly temperatures for 30 years [both displayed]. Which data set shows weather? Which shows climate? Explain the difference using specific data points.”
Scripted → High Guided
8
Q13 — Trait Variation Recall
Replace with evidence-based reasoning.
Before: “Variations in traits between individuals in a population...”
After: “Scientists measured beak length in 50 finches on an island. During a drought year, only seeds with hard shells remained. Finches with longer beaks were more successful at cracking these seeds [data graph]. Based on this data, explain how trait variation can affect survival.”
Scripted → High Guided
9
Q16 — Habitat Parts
Replace with ecosystem data analysis.
Before: “The parts of a habitat...”
After: “A local pond ecosystem includes algae, tadpoles, fish, and herons [food web diagram + population data]. If a disease killed most of the algae, predict what would happen to each population. Use the food web to explain the chain of effects.”
Scripted → High Guided
10
Q21 — Static Electricity
Replace with investigation data.
Before: “What causes hair to stand up when you rub a balloon on it?”
After: “Students rubbed different materials together and tested if they attracted small pieces of paper [data table showing material pairs and results]. Based on the data, which material combinations created the strongest attraction? What pattern do you notice about which materials produce static charge?”
Scripted → High Guided

Projected Impact

This test requires the most extensive revision of any G3 assessment. Converting the 21 Scripted recall items to evidence-based questions would shift the complexity from 66%/28%/6%/0% to approximately 0%/45%/40%/15% — meeting all four Achieve targets. The dramatic improvement shown by Q2 and Q30 (the only items with experimental data) proves that even simple data tables transform recall questions into genuine reasoning tasks. Priority: Replace all definition/vocabulary recall items with data-driven tasks.
