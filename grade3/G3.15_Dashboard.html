What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK Level 1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Student applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Student applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. No single right path. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

Issues with construct redundancy and topic balance
  • LS Overrepresentation (55% vs. 44% target)
    12 of 22 questions are Life Science, exceeding the NGSS target by 11 percentage points. Reduce LS items to make room for underrepresented domains.
  • ESS Underrepresentation (9% vs. 17% target)
    Only 2 ESS questions (Q11, Q12). Both are Strong items using climate data, but the domain needs more coverage.
  • ETS Underrepresentation (9% vs. 17% target)
    Only 2 ETS questions (Q13, Q20). Use freed LS slots to add engineering design items with trade-off analysis.
  • Multiple Life Cycle Recall Items Clustered at Start (Q1, Q2, Q3 all LS1-1)
    Three consecutive life cycle questions at the start of the test, all targeting the same standard. Q1 and Q3 are pure recall. Consolidate into one data-driven life cycle question.
  • Strong Climate Data Cluster (Q11-Q12) Shows Effective Use of Real Data
    The Mexico City climate data questions are the highlight of this test. Both use authentic data for prediction and graph interpretation. Use as a model for upgrading recall items.
