G3.2: The Strongest G3 Test — But Still Split

G3.2 has 9 Strong questions (30%) — more than any other G3 test. It includes outstanding items: the toy truck force experiment (Q3-4), salvia plant investigation (Q18-20), frog habitat engineering (Q28), and lizard natural selection (Q30). However, it also has 12 Scripted items (40%), many of which are exact duplicates from other tests (Q5, Q8, Q10, Q15).

What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

  • Cross-Test Duplicates — Q5, Q8, Q10, Q12, Q14, Q15
    Six questions are exact or near-exact duplicates from G3.12. This indicates a shared question bank being reused without modification. These should be replaced with unique items.
  • LS Over-representation — 19 of 30 questions (63%)
    Life Science dominates this test at 19% above NGSS target. Remove inherited/learned behavior duplicates and vocabulary items to free slots for ESS and ETS.
  • Vocabulary Recall — Q11 (germination), Q26 (trait definition)
    Pure vocabulary questions have no place on NGSS-aligned assessments. Replace with phenomenon-driven items.

Bright Spots: Model Questions for Other Tests

Q3-4 (toy truck force data), Q18-20 (salvia investigation), Q28 (frog habitat engineering), Q29 (magnet paper holder design), and Q30 (lizard camouflage data) are among the best G3 questions across all tests. These should be used as templates for redesigning weaker items on other tests.
