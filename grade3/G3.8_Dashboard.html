Achieve Standard: 0% DOK1 Items

This assessment has 37% DOK1 items — 11 of 30 questions are Scripted recall. The Low Guided distribution (33%) falls below the 40-50% target, while the High Guided distribution (30%) meets the 20-30% target. The test lacks any Doing Science items. Nine questions reach Strong rating, anchored by excellent data-driven items including Q7 (cow milk production experiment), Q10 (peppered moth natural selection), and Q19-Q21 (forces investigation sequence). However, the test includes three vocabulary recall items (Q23, Q24, Q25) scoring 1.0 average and two consecutive life cycle recall items (Q1-Q2). Life Science dominates at 53%, exceeding the 44% NGSS target, while Engineering is critically underrepresented at only 3% (1 question vs. 17% target).

What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK Level 1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Student applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Student applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. No single right path. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

Issues with construct redundancy and topic balance
  • LS Overrepresentation (53% vs. 44% target)
    16 of 30 questions are Life Science, exceeding the NGSS target by 9 percentage points. The LS questions span six different standards (LS1-1, LS2-1, LS3-1, LS3-2, LS4-1, LS4-2, LS4-3, LS4-4), providing good standard variety within the domain, but overall LS count should be reduced.
  • ETS Critical Underrepresentation (3% vs. 17% target)
    Only 1 ETS question (Q26 — foil boat engineering competition). The test needs 4-5 engineering design items to approach the NGSS target. Use freed LS slots to add engineering design problems with trade-off analysis and iterative testing.
  • Three Vocabulary Recall Items Score 1.0 (Q23, Q24, Q25)
    Three consecutive questions test pure vocabulary definitions (magnetism, repel, force on a ball). These score 1.0 across all indicators — the lowest possible. Vocabulary items do not assess science understanding and should be replaced with data-driven investigations.
  • Two Consecutive LS1-1 Recall Items at Start (Q1, Q2)
    The test opens with two back-to-back life cycle questions targeting the same standard (3-LS1-1), both Scripted at 1.25 average. Consolidate into one evidence-based life cycle question.
  • Strong Forces Investigation Sequence (Q19-Q21)
    Q19 (tug-of-war prediction), Q20 (balanced forces design), and Q21a/Q21b (Newton's cradle data analysis and pattern extension) form an excellent connected investigation sequence. All are High Guided/Strong. Use as a model for other domains.
  • Q7 (Cow Milk Production) Is the Highest-Scoring Item (3.5 avg)
    Q7 presents a rich experimental scenario with controlled variables, multiple data tables, and requires students to analyze pre/post production data to identify the environmental variable. This item exemplifies what the Achieve framework targets.
