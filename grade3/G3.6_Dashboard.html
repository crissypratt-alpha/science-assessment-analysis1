Achieve Standard: 0% DOK1 Items

This assessment has 63% DOK1 items — 19 of 30 questions are Scripted recall. The Low Guided distribution (17%) falls well short of the 40-50% target, and the test lacks any Doing Science items. Six questions reach High Guided or above (Q15 using Auckland climate data, Q23 using doghouse insulation engineering, Q24 on Indian Pipe plant traits, Q27 on moose antler inheritance evidence, Q29 using Miami-Portland climate comparison, and Q30 on Pterosaur fossil analysis). Life Science dominates at 60%, far exceeding the 44% NGSS target, while Engineering & Technology has zero representation. Group behavior recall items (Q4, Q10, Q16, Q17) and adaptation identification items (Q5, Q7, Q14, Q22, Q25) form heavy clusters of Scripted content.

What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK Level 1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Student applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Student applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. No single right path. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

Issues with construct redundancy and topic balance
  • LS Overrepresentation (60% vs. 44% target)
    18 of 30 questions are Life Science, exceeding the NGSS target by 16 percentage points. Seven questions target LS4-3 (adaptations) alone (Q5, Q7, Q11, Q13, Q14, Q22, Q25), creating heavy redundancy in structural adaptation recall.
  • ETS Completely Missing (0% vs. 17% target)
    Zero ETS questions despite the NGSS target of 17%. While Q6 (magnetic box) and Q23 (doghouse insulation) touch engineering design, both are classified under PS2-3 and ESS3-1 respectively. Dedicated ETS items with explicit engineering design constraints are needed.
  • PS Underrepresentation (17% vs. 22% target)
    Only 5 PS questions. Three target PS2-3 (magnets/forces) and two target PS2-1 (balanced forces). No coverage of other physical science standards.
  • Four Group Behavior Recall Items (Q4, Q10, Q16, Q17)
    Four questions target the same standard (3-LS2-1) with nearly identical Scripted complexity. All ask students to identify or classify group behavior with no data or evidence. Consolidate into one evidence-based group behavior analysis.
  • Seven LS4-3 Adaptation Recall Items (Q5, Q7, Q11, Q13, Q14, Q22, Q25)
    Seven questions test simple adaptation identification at the Scripted level. This represents 23% of the entire test on a single standard with no progression in complexity. Reduce to 2-3 items and elevate with comparative data.
  • Six Strong Questions Demonstrate Effective Assessment Design
    Q15 (Auckland climate with dual constraints), Q23 (doghouse insulation engineering), Q24 (Indian Pipe plant traits), Q27 (moose antler inheritance evidence), Q29 (Miami-Portland climate comparison), and Q30 (Pterosaur fossil analysis) all use real-world data or rich scenarios to drive genuine reasoning. Use as models for upgrading recall items.
