Achieve Standard: 0% DOK1 Items

This assessment has 23% DOK1 items — 5 of 22 scored items are Scripted recall. While this is the lowest Scripted proportion among evaluated G3 tests, the assessment still exceeds the 0% target. The High Guided distribution (45%) significantly exceeds the 20–30% target, which is a positive indicator of cognitive rigor. However, the test lacks any Doing Science items (target: 5–10%). Physical Science dominates at 59%, far exceeding the 22% NGSS target, while Life Science sits at 41%, close to the 44% target. No Earth & Space Science or Engineering questions appear in the evaluated portion. The strongest cluster is Q1–Q9 (forces and magnets investigations), all rated High Guided or Strong.

What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK Level 1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Student applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Student applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. No single right path. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

Issues with domain balance, construct coverage, and missing content areas
  • PS Overrepresentation (59% vs. 22% target)
    13 of 22 items are Physical Science (forces and magnets) — nearly triple the NGSS target. The first 12 questions are all PS, creating a domain-heavy front section. While these PS items are generally strong (8 of 13 rated Strong), the test needs rebalancing.
  • ESS Completely Missing (0% vs. 17% target)
    No Earth & Space Science questions appear in Q1–Q21. The unevaluated Q22–Q40 section may contain ESS items, but this cannot be confirmed without text extraction.
  • ETS Completely Missing (0% vs. 17% target)
    No Engineering & Technology questions appear in Q1–Q21, though Q9 (magnet cabinet door) involves engineering application within a PS standard. Add standalone ETS items with trade-off analysis and design constraints.
  • Five Scripted Items Concentrated in Late Questions
    Q11, Q12, Q13, Q16, and Q20 are all Scripted recall — four of these five fall in the Q11–Q20 range. These items lack scenarios, data, and crosscutting concept engagement. Each could be elevated by adding investigation data and requiring pattern analysis.
  • Strong PS Investigation Cluster (Q1–Q9)
    Questions 1–9 form an exceptionally strong cluster using force diagrams, tug-of-war scenarios, Newton's cradle data tables, toy truck experiments, and magnetic force investigations. Q4 (Newton's cradle experimental design) scores the highest at 3.5 avg. Use these as models for upgrading weaker items.
  • Q19 (Plant Breeding Data) Is the Only Strong LS Item
    Q19 uses breeding experiment data to test inheritance concepts — the only LS question rated Strong. This demonstrates that Life Science items can reach High Guided when paired with experimental data. Use as a model for upgrading Q13, Q16, and Q20.

MAP Alignment Recommendations — Before & After
1
Q11 — Magnetic Mixture Separation (Scripted → High Guided)
Replace recall identification with experimental data analysis.
Before: "Brad and Brian must separate a mixture. Which items can be removed with a magnet and why?"
After: "Students tested whether a magnet could attract each item in a mixture and recorded results [data table: item, material, attracted yes/no]. Based on the data, what pattern explains which items are magnetic? Predict whether a nickel coin would be attracted and explain your reasoning."
Scripted → High Guided
2
Q12 — Magnet Problem Solving (Scripted → High Guided)
Replace 3-choice recall with multi-criteria engineering problem.
Before: "Which problem could be solved by a magnet?" (only 3 answer choices)
After: "Engineers tested four different tools for holding a cabinet door closed [data table: tool, holding strength, cost, ease of install, reusable]. Based on the data, which tool best meets all the requirements? What trade-offs would you accept if cost is the most important factor?"
Scripted → High Guided
3
Q13 — Butterfly Life Cycle Order (Scripted → High Guided)
Replace sequence recall with evidence-based life cycle analysis.
Before: "Which order shows the correct life cycle of a butterfly?"
After: "A student observed changes in an unfamiliar insect over 8 weeks and recorded observations [data table: week, size, appearance, behavior]. Based on the data, identify each life cycle stage and explain what evidence shows the transition between stages."
Scripted → High Guided
4
Q16 — Wild Horse Herd Benefit (Scripted → High Guided)
Replace recall with evidence-based survival data analysis.
Before: "How does living in a herd help wild horses?"
After: "Scientists tracked wild horse survival rates over three years [data table: group size, predator encounters, survival rate, food found per day]. Based on the data, what pattern describes how herd size affects survival? What is one trade-off of living in a larger herd?"
Scripted → High Guided
5
Q20 — Inherited vs. Acquired Traits (Scripted → High Guided)
Replace recall identification with data-driven classification.
Before: "Which trait is least likely to be passed on from parents to children?"
After: "Scientists compared traits of 20 parent-offspring pairs [data table: trait, parent value, offspring value, match rate]. Based on the data, which traits show an inheritance pattern and which do not? What evidence supports your classification?"
Scripted → High Guided
6
Q3 — Balanced Forces Evidence (Low Guided → High Guided)
Strengthen claim-evidence structure with experimental data.
Before: "Which example best provides evidence that balanced forces combine to produce a net force of zero?"
After: "Students measured forces on four objects and recorded whether each moved [data table: object, Force A direction/amount, Force B direction/amount, movement result]. Which objects show balanced forces? Use the force data to explain why those objects did not move."
Low Guided → High Guided
7
Q10 — Magnet Game Design (Low Guided → High Guided)
Add experimental data to evaluate design approaches.
Before: "Alex wants to build a simple game where a toy car moves across a track without being touched. How can you design the game using magnets?"
After: "Students tested four ways to move a toy car without touching it [data table: method, distance moved, reliability, materials needed]. Based on the data, which method works best? What pattern do you see between magnet distance and car movement?"
Low Guided → High Guided
8
Q14 — Ladybug Life Cycle Stage (Low Guided → High Guided)
Add developmental timing data to deepen analysis.
Before: "The diagram shows four numbered stages in the life cycle of a ladybug. Which process must occur between stage 3 and stage 4?"
After: "Scientists recorded ladybug development over 6 weeks [data table: week, stage number, size, description of changes]. Based on the data, what process occurs between stage 3 and stage 4? How does the data show this is development rather than growth?"
Low Guided → High Guided
9
Q18 — Raspberry Color Inheritance (Low Guided → Doing Science)
Add breeding data and require investigation design.
Before: "Which best describes how raspberries get their color?"
After: "A farmer noticed some yellow raspberries growing from red raspberry plants. She bred different color combinations [data table: parent 1 color, parent 2 color, offspring colors]. Based on the data, is raspberry color inherited or caused by the environment? Design an experiment to test whether sunlight affects raspberry color."
Low Guided → Doing Science

Q21a — Tree Height: Environment vs. Inheritance (Low Guided → Doing Science)

Based on the data, is tree height determined by environment, inheritance, or both? Design an investigation to test which factor has the greatest effect."
