Achieve Standard: 0% DOK1 Items

This assessment has 32% DOK1 items — 11 of 34 questions are Scripted recall. While this is lower than many G3 tests, it still far exceeds the Achieve target of 0%. The Low Guided distribution (47%) hits the 40-50% target well, and High Guided (21%) reaches the lower bound of the 20-30% target. However, the test has zero Doing Science items. Notable strengths include the ReadWorks passage cluster (Q4a-4e) which probes genetics at multiple cognitive levels, and the multi-source data integration in Q30a-30b (salvia plant growth). Heavy reliance on reading comprehension passages (ReadWorks) means several items assess literacy more than science. Life Science dominates at 53%, exceeding the 44% NGSS target, while ESS is severely underrepresented at just 6%.

What Do the Complexity Levels Mean?

From Achieve's Framework to Evaluate the Cognitive Complexity of Science Assessment Tasks (2019).

Scripted (Recall)
Student recalls a fact or definition. No reasoning. DOK Level 1. Target: 0%.
Low Guided (Application)
Student applies a concept to a familiar scenario. Some reasoning. Target: 40-50%.
High Guided (Transfer)
Student applies understanding to unfamiliar situations with data/evidence. Target: 20-30%.
Doing Science (Authentic)
Genuine scientific reasoning with novel data. No single right path. Target: 5-10%.

Content Coverage Flags

Issues with construct redundancy, topic balance, and reading comprehension dependency
  • Heavy ReadWorks Passage Dependency (Q4a-4e, Q13-14, Q15, Q18-19, Q21-22)
    At least 12 of 34 questions rely on reading comprehension passages from ReadWorks. While passages provide rich context (Scenario avg 2.5), several questions assess literacy skills rather than science understanding. Q4e (main idea) and Q21 (vocabulary: "damp") are reading comprehension items, not science assessment items.
  • ESS Severe Underrepresentation (6% vs. 17% target)
    Only 2 ESS questions (Q6 weather map, Q29 roof wind design). Q6 is a basic map-reading Scripted item while Q29 is a Strong engineering-design question. The domain needs 4-5 more items covering weather patterns, climate, and natural hazards.
  • ETS Underrepresentation (9% vs. 17% target)
    Only 3 ETS questions (Q11, Q12, Q24). Q11 and Q12 are both biomimicry trivia (Scripted) and Q24 is design-process recall. None reach High Guided. Replace biomimicry trivia with data-driven engineering design tasks.
  • PS Overrepresentation (32% vs. 22% target)
    11 Physical Science questions, 10 percentage points above the NGSS target. Reduce PS items (especially the 3 Scripted magnetic force items Q1-Q2, Q23) to make room for ESS and ETS coverage.
  • Multi-Part Question Cluster Q4a-4e Is a Model for Passage-Based Assessment
    The puppy genetics passage (Q4a-4e) progresses from recall to evidence-based reasoning. Q4c reaches High Guided by asking students to draw conclusions from textual evidence about gene expression. This scaffolded approach is effective when the questions genuinely assess science rather than reading comprehension.
  • Q30a-30b (Salvia Multi-Source Data) Is the Strongest Item on the Test
    This two-part question requires integrating experimental data with two real-world climate graphs to predict optimal growing conditions and justify with evidence. With indicator averages of 3.5 each, these are the highest-complexity items on the assessment. Use as the model for redesigning Scripted items.
  • Q2 Has Only 2 Answer Choices — Below Technical Standard
    Q2 (magnet repelling) offers only Image 1 or Image 2 as choices, giving students a 50% guess rate. This is below the standard 4-choice MC format and inflates guessing probability.

Q30a-b's three-source data synthesis and Q22's context-dependent adaptation reasoning should serve as models for upgrading recall items. Addressing the reading comprehension dependency by adding quantitative data to passage-based items would strengthen the science assessment validity.
