Key Takeaways

MAP Science covers everything NGSS requires
MAP Science 3-5 is aligned to ALL 16 Grade 5 NGSS performance expectations (plus Grades 3-4). It is adaptive and draws from the full standard. Students will encounter questions on every domain and standard.
The MobyMax Skill Plan is comprehensive
With 120 lessons covering 13 of 16 PEs fully, the skill plan is strong across Life Science (44 lessons), Earth Science (33 lessons), Physical Science (35 lessons), and Space (8 lessons). Only the 3 engineering design standards lack explicit coverage.
G5.1 test has gaps: ~5 standards missing
Water distribution (5-ESS2-2) is completely absent. All 3 engineering design standards (3-5-ETS1-1/2/3) have zero questions. The particle model of matter (5-PS1-1) and gravity (5-PS2-1) are unconfirmed. Students could pass this test and still fail MAP on these standards.
G5.1 is badly imbalanced: 44% of questions cover 3 standards
14 of 32 questions (44%) test just 3 standards: matter cycling/food webs 5-LS2-1 (5 Qs), Earth's spheres 5-ESS2-1 (5 Qs), and shadows/day-night 5-ESS1-2 (4 Qs). Meanwhile, 5 entire standards get zero confirmed questions.
Engineering design completely absent from test despite MAP assessing it
None of the 32 questions address engineering design (3-5-ETS1-1, ETS1-2, or ETS1-3). MAP Science will test these standards. The skill plan only partially covers them through implicit project work. This is the weakest link across all three sources.
Particle model of matter (5-PS1-1) likely undertested
5-PS1-1 is foundational to Grade 5 physical science — understanding that matter is made of particles too small to be seen. Despite being well-taught in the skill plan, it has no confirmed test questions. This standard is a building block for middle school chemistry concepts.
