Key Takeaways

MAP Science 6-8 covers all MS NGSS PEs
MAP Science 6-8 is aligned to ALL middle school NGSS performance expectations. Since MS standards are banded across Grades 6-8 (not grade-specific), the adaptive test draws from the full MS standard set. Students will encounter questions on every domain and standard.
The 6-8 skill plan is robust with 433 lessons
MS Biology is especially strong with 144 lessons covering cells, body systems, photosynthesis, and evolution. Chemistry (68 lessons), Physics (64 lessons), and Earth & Space (75 lessons) provide solid coverage. 15 of 17 G6-focus PEs are taught through the single 6-8 band.
G6.1 test is relatively balanced but still missing 4 standards
Unlike the heavily skewed G3 and G4 tests, G6.1 distributes questions more evenly across life science, physical science, and earth science. However, photosynthesis (MS-LS1-6), waves (MS-PS4-2), human population impact (MS-ESS3-4), and design problem definition (MS-ETS1-1) are completely absent.
Photosynthesis/matter-energy cycling (MS-LS1-6) completely untested
This is a core life science standard about photosynthesis and the cycling of matter and flow of energy. Despite being taught in the 144-lesson biology unit and assessed on MAP, the G6.1 test has zero questions on it. Students could have no understanding of this foundational concept and the test would not detect it.
Waves (MS-PS4-2) untested despite MAP assessing it
The wave model standard — how waves are reflected, absorbed, or transmitted through materials — is not tested on G6.1 and only partially covered in the skill plan. MAP will assess this. Students may be underprepared in this area.
Only 21 questions total — fewer than G3 (30) or G4 (31)
With only 21 questions covering 17 target PEs, the G6.1 test has about 1.2 questions per standard on average. This limits the breadth of coverage achievable and means some standards will inevitably go untested. Consider whether the question count should be expanded for better coverage.
